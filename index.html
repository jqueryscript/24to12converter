Frequently Asked Questions

What is 00:00 in 12-hour format?

00:00 in 24-hour format is 12:00 AM in 12-hour format.

What is 12:00 in 12-hour format?

12:00 in 24-hour format is 12:00 PM in 12-hour format.

Is 24:00 a valid time?

While some contexts use it to signify the end of a day, 24:00 is technically not a valid time in the standard 24-hour format. The day ends at 23:59 and the next day starts at 00:00.

What is 24-hour time?

24-hour time, also known as military time, is a timekeeping convention where the day runs from midnight to midnight and is divided into 24 hours. It is indicated by the hours (00-23) and minutes (00-59) without AM/PM designations. For example, 1 PM is 13:00, and 5 PM is 17:00.

Understanding 24-hour vs. 12-hour clocks

The primary difference between 24-hour and 12-hour clocks lies in how they represent the hours of a day. The 12-hour clock uses AM (ante meridiem, before noon) and PM (post meridiem, after noon) to distinguish between the first and second halves of the day, with hours cycling from 1 to 12. The 24-hour clock, conversely, uses a continuous count from 00 to 23, eliminating the need for AM/PM and reducing ambiguity, especially in fields like aviation, military, and medicine.

Why convert 24-hour time to 12-hour?

Converting 24-hour time to 12-hour format is often necessary for clarity and common understanding in regions that primarily use the 12-hour system (like the United States, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand). It helps in scheduling, communication, and daily life to ensure everyone understands the exact time without confusion, especially when dealing with events or appointments.

How to convert 24-hour time to 12-hour format?

To convert 24-hour time to 12-hour format, follow these simple rules: For hours 00 to 11, keep the hour as is and add "AM" (00:00 becomes 12:00 AM). For 12:00, it's 12:00 PM. For hours 13 to 23, subtract 12 from the hour and add "PM" (e.g., 13:00 becomes 1:00 PM, 20:00 becomes 8:00 PM). Our converter automates this process for you.

Understanding AM vs. PM

AM (ante meridiem) stands for "before midday" and PM (post meridiem) stands for "after midday." In the 12-hour format, AM denotes the time from midnight (12:00 AM) to noon (11:59 AM), while PM denotes the time from noon (12:00 PM) to midnight (11:59 PM). This system is essential for distinguishing between morning and afternoon/evening hours.

How to Convert 24-Hour to 12-Hour Time Manually

Manual conversion is straightforward. For hours 0-11, add AM (e.g., 09:00 is 9:00 AM), with 00:00 being a special case (12:00 AM). For hour 12, add PM (12:00 is 12:00 PM). For hours 13-23, subtract 12 and add PM (e.g., 14:00 becomes 2:00 PM). Minutes remain the same.
